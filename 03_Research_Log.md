# Purpose

This document serves as the chronological record of all research conducted for the project.

Every article, blog, Reddit discussion, LinkedIn post, YouTube video, community conversation, or personal experiment that contributes to the project should be recorded here.

The purpose of this log is to:

- Track where ideas originate.
- Validate engineering problems with evidence.
- Avoid duplicate research.
- Record new solutions and best practices.
- Build confidence before introducing product features.

This document records **research history**, not detailed problem descriptions. Detailed problems belong in the Master Problem Database.

# Research Timeline

## Update 01

Initial project vision established.

Focused on understanding whether the biggest opportunity in AI-assisted development lies outside code generation.

---

## Update 02

Collected early developer pain points around:

- project planning
- prompt engineering
- context loss
- security

Validated that many developer frustrations occur before or after code generation.

---

## Update 03

Research shifted toward AI-assisted software engineering rather than AI-assisted coding.

This became the foundation of the project vision.

---

## Update 04

Identified repeated community discussions around context window limitations, token usage, and long-term project memory.

These findings later evolved into the Context Management research theme.

---

## Update 05

Research highlighted that developers frequently struggle with choosing tools, frameworks, and third-party services.

Confirmed the need for recommendation and decision-support features.

---

## Update 06

Discovered recurring concerns around architecture, scalability, and maintainability.

Reinforced the importance of engineering guidance over code generation.

---

## Update 07

Community discussions emphasized that many AI-generated projects fail due to weak planning rather than poor implementation.

---

## Update 08

Collected multiple examples of state drift, technical debt, and context degradation during large AI-assisted projects.

---

## Update 09

Research expanded into security, deployment readiness, and production engineering concerns.

---

## Update 10

Multiple sources confirmed that beginners often reinvent existing solutions instead of using mature services and libraries.

---

## Update 11

Identified design-related issues including repetitive interfaces, default icon libraries, and generic AI-generated visual patterns.

---

## Update 12

Collected enterprise perspectives highlighting governance, reliability, and maintainability challenges for AI-assisted development.

---

## Update 13

Confirmed that prompt quality alone does not solve engineering problems.

Structured workflows consistently outperform isolated prompts.

---

## Update 14

Research increasingly pointed toward a broader opportunity:

Helping developers make engineering decisions rather than generating code.

This significantly refined the product vision.

---

## Update 15

Current research confirms that the largest remaining bottlenecks in AI-assisted software development are:

- planning
- architecture
- engineering decisions
- verification
- context management
- security
- cost optimization
- project knowledge

These findings directly support the long-term vision of an AI Software Engineering Companion.

# Research Principles

- Evidence before implementation.
- Multiple independent sources increase confidence.
- Every problem should be traceable to research.
- Every feature should map to one or more validated problems.
- Research remains an ongoing activity throughout the project lifecycle.
